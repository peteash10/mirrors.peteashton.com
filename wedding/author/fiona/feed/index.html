While there will be a ton of special people at the wedding, my parents won’t be there, which brings a bit of a lump to my throat as I write this. Dad died in 1996 and Mum in 2001.

Thanks to an idea from my niece Hana, they will be represented at the wedding in the form of special wedding confetti, which I’ve been hand-making from the flowers in Mum’s garden. Both of their ashes are in the garden and I’m sure that’s a reason that the garden is so beautiful.

So, since March when the camellia and magnolia first bloomed, through May’s rhododendron blossoms to the current explosion of roses, I’ve become a confetti-manufacturer. (Two mins either side in the microwave, if you’re interested.) Of course they have turned out to be absolutely beautiful so I thought I’d share some of the pictures before these petals are scattered to wind. Although I’ve made so much I’m thinking of setting up a side business.

]]> Killing time in Brum? http://ash10.com/wedding/2014/06/17/killing-time-in-brum/ Tue, 17 Jun 2014 20:48:30 +0000 http://ash10.com/wedding/?p=135 Continue reading Killing time in Brum? →]]> Hey! If you have time to kill between ceremony and reception, or you’re arriving early to get checked into a hotel, then here are some suggestions of things to do. Email me if you want more tips – these are in the vicinity of the registry office but there’s a lot more to see.

1. More canals than Venice
Ah Birmingham – the Venice of the Midlands. There are canal boat rides going on fairly regularly over the weekend, if you fancy taking a trip. Looks like there is an hour’s trip on Saturday at 4pm that might be the perfect time-waster.

Visit: http://www.sherbornewharf.co.uk/trips.htm

2. Library of Birmingham
The new Library of Birmingham opened last year – there are rooftop gardens on different layers of the library cake, good views over the city, a photography café, egg chairs, music library and listening posts, and the transplanted Shakespeare Room at the top. The space inside the book tower is brill too.

Visit:  http://www.libraryofbirmingham.com

3. Modern art is rubbish – discuss.
The IKON gallery is in Brindleyplace and close to the canals. It’s Brum’s contemporary art gallery with ever-changing exhibitions. Tip: go up to the top in the lift – it’s a Martin Creed art piece. I’ll say no more. Then wander down through the galleries.

Visit: http://ikon-gallery.org

4. Traditional art is rubbish – discuss.
BMAG (Birmingham Museum and Art Gallery) is known for its PreRaphaelite collection and being the home of the Staffordshire Hoard, a 7th-century treasure trove of gold pieces found by a dude with a metal detector a couple of years ago.  Check out the glorious Edwardian Tea Room for a sit-down and a cuppa. Mostly free, but it might be worth seeing the Rowland Emmett exhibition for £5 – it’s been getting good headlines up here.

Visit: http://www.bmag.org.uk

5. Mailbox for munchies
The Mailbox is at the back of the registry office and is full of tasty eating options. I like Bar Epernay especially if the pianist is playing and Bar Estilo for the tapas. There’s a Harvey Nics in there somewhere too for shopping fiends or people who just like to gasp and be appalled at price tags.

Visit: http://www.mailboxlife.com

6. Towpath to Bournville train station
If it’s a lovely day, you can race the boat. Only four miles and a lovely walk that even goes across a little aqueduct just by the University of Birmingham. Or take the train instead and wander around Cadbury’s – or Cadbury World (it’s free to go in the shop).

Visit: http://www.cadburyworld.co.uk
